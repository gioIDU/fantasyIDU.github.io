Introduction

What is fantasy basketball?

Fantasy is a game which can be played across multiple professional sports of the US. Yahoo, ESPN & CBS all host fantasy basketball. Fantasy basketball is a season long game. We are going to focus solely on fantasy basketball for this example. The fantasy season starts after the draft and finishes at the end of the NBA season (not including playoffs). In fantasy, people select NBA players through the draft. The objective of the game is to pick players to help collect the most fruitful statistic line, trying to lead in every category. The main categories that most people use are points, steals, blocks, turnovers, assists, field goal percentage, the number of threes, & rebounds (categories vary). You are given a score per category depend on how you rank (rotisserie). The idea is to put together a well-rounded team in every statistic aspect. To do so, it will require a lot of number crunching, calculations, and research.

How does the draft work?

A draft is the time where you, the manager has to select the basketball players that will be in your future fantasy team. There are two types of drafts that are used to select player, a snake draft and an auction draft. In a snake draft, the first round is randomly ordered and then in the next round the order is reversed. (For example you drafted last in the first round you would be drafting first in the second round). In a fantasy draft there are on average 12 rounds in the draft (this can vary), each round you are able to draft one current NBA player.
